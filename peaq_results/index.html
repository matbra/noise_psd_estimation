PEAQ Examples

This section reproduces a diagram similar to Figure 6 in the article. You can click on the points and listen to the signals that the ODG values are based on.

Due to copyright issues these signals are not the same as those used for the evaluation that is presented in the article. Please note that the results presented here differ somewhat from the results presented in the article. This is caused by different characteristics of the clean signals, with the signals presented here having considerably more transient elements and high frequency content than the comparatively old signals that are used for the evaluation in the article. The clean signals that this evaluation is based on have been downloaded from jamendo and have all been published under a creative commons Attribution license.

The upper row of tabs contains the results for two impulse removal algorithms: LSAR, which is used in the article, and DT-LSAR.

The following tabs contain the diagrams for the two SNR concepts - gain and impulse density - and additionally for two real-world disturbances, i.e., a recording of the blank groove of an 78 RPM shellac disc and a manually spliced recording of vinyl noise.
