Abstract

This article presents a new algorithm to estimate the power spectral density of stationary broadband noise disturbances in audio recordings. The developed estimation algorithm is an extension of quantile-based estimation algorithms that takes into account assumptions about the distribution of short-time Fourier coefficients of the noise disturbance. Evaluation with a large number of test signals shows that a high detection accuracy can be obtained, outperforming state-of-the-art algorithms, allowing for unsupervised operation and thus constituting an important part of a fully automatic broadband noise disturbance restoration system.
